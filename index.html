WHO
I AM

I am a second-year dual degree student at IIIT Hyderabad, pursuing a B.Tech in Computer Science and an MS in Computational Natural Sciences.

At the CCNSB lab, I work as an undergraduate researcher under Prof. Chittaranjan Hens, investigating how signed edge weights in complex networks affect structural balance and the conditions under which these systems undergo irreversible dynamical phase transitions. My current work connects percolation theory to the onset of large-scale connectivity collapse in network models.

My foundation is competitive mathematics. I qualified for the Indian National Mathematical Olympiad and attended the INMO Training Camp at ICTS-TIFR in 2022. I actively train in competitive programming and build systems that apply mathematical reasoning to real computational problems.

I also have a deep passion for the engineering behind superbikes, constantly analyzing the limits of their mechanics and performance.
